3. How the App Provides "Emergency Help" for Legal Situations
The app handles sensitive or confusing queries through a hybrid intelligence model:
1. Intent Classification (Layer 1): User input (e.g., a natural language query about a police encounter or a specific complaint) is processed by a lightweight, on-device machine learning model (like Apple's MLTextClassifier). This model's purpose is to quickly map the user's input to a predefined intent, such as legal.police_encounter. This classification is extremely fast, taking only 3–10 milliseconds.
2. Narrative Engine (Layer 2): For high-stakes situations identified by the classifier, the app launches a pre-authored conversation tree using the Ink scripting language. This is a deterministic (non-generative) guide that leads the user through pre-verified steps and procedures, eliminating the risk of AI hallucination. This system is suitable for guiding a user through an interaction where they need clear, step-by-step procedural advice.
3. Knowledge Engine (Layer 3): If the query is complex or involves looking up detailed legal sections, the app uses hybrid semantic and keyword search (SQLite with sqlite-vec and FTS5) to retrieve the exact text from the bundled legal datasets.
In essence, the app is architected to include legal and procedural content—designed to be authoritative and non-ambiguous—which can be delivered either as essential pre-bundled data or as modular downloads, fitting the specific needs you outlined.
