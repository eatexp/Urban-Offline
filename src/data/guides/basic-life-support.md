# Basic Life Support (BLS)

**Source:** Aligned with ILCOR / WHO Guidelines (2025)

## 1. Safety First (Scene Safety)
Before approaching a victim, ensure the scene is safe for YOU.
- Check for: Fire, electrical hazards, gas, traffic, or structural instability.
- **Rule:** If the scene is unsafe, do not enter. Call for help.

## 2. Check Responsiveness
- Tap the victim's shoulders firmly.
- Shout: "Are you okay?"
- **If NO response:** Treat as cardiac arrest until proven otherwise.

## 3. Call for Help
- Point to a specific person: "YOU, call emergency services (911/999) and get an AED."
- If alone, call immediately on speakerphone.

## 4. Check Breathing & Pulse
- **Look:** Is the chest rising?
- **Listen:** For breath sounds (gasping is NOT normal breathing).
- **Feel:** Check carotid pulse (neck) for max 10 seconds.
- **Action:** If no breathing/pulse -> **Start CPR**.

## 5. CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)
**Ratio:** 30 Compressions : 2 Breaths

### Compressions
- **Hand Position:** Center of the chest (lower half of sternum).
- **Depth:** At least 2 inches (5 cm).
- **Rate:** 100-120 per minute (Tempo: "Stayin' Alive").
- **Recoil:** Let chest fully recoil between pushes.

### Breaths (If trained)
- Tilt head back, lift chin.
- Pinch nose.
- Give 2 breaths (1 second each). Watch chest rise.
- *Note: If untrained or no barrier device, perform Hands-Only CPR.*

## 6. AED (Automated External Defibrillator)
- Turn it on immediately.
- Follow voice prompts.
- Attach pads to bare chest (as shown on pads).
- **Clear** the patient when analyzing or shocking.
